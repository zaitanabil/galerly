## Brand Essence

### Core Philosophy

**"Share art, not files"**

Galerly exists at the intersection of artistry and technology. The brand transforms photography sharing from a technical file-transfer process into an emotional, beautiful experience. Every interaction should feel crafted, considered, and elegantâ€”never rushed, generic, or transactional.

### Brand Personality

- **Elegant** but not pretentious
- **Simple** but not simplistic  
- **Professional** but not cold
- **Modern** but not trendy
- **Direct** but not blunt
- **Confident** but not arrogant

### Audience Focus

**Primary:** Professional photographers who care deeply about how their work is presented  
**Secondary:** Photography clients who experience galleries as viewers and downloaders  
**Tone toward both:** Respectful, honest, straightforward

## Voice & Tone

### Voice Characteristics

#### Direct & Honest
- Short sentences. No fluff.
- Say what it is. Mean what you say.
- "Drag. Drop. Done." not "Simply drag and effortlessly drop your files"
- "Your work stays yours." not "We provide industry-leading intellectual property protection"

#### Confident & Understated
- Let the work speak. Don't oversell.
- "Clean lines. Full resolution." not "Amazing revolutionary gallery design"
- "Time is precious." not "We save you so much valuable time"
- State facts. Avoid hype.

#### Human & Approachable
- Write like a skilled craftsperson talking to another craftsperson
- "We're here to help." not "Our customer success team is standing by"
- "Questions? Ask us anything." not "For inquiries, please consult our comprehensive FAQ"
- Natural rhythm. Conversational but not casual.

#### Aspirational but Grounded
- "Your artistry deserves this." (aspirational)
- "Upload fast. Download faster." (grounded in reality)
- Balance emotion with function
- Never promise what you can't deliver

### Tone Across Contexts

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Marketing** | Inspiring, elegant | "Where moments become memories" |
| **Product Features** | Clear, functional | "Upload in seconds. Full resolution preserved." |
| **Help & Support** | Warm, patient | "Tell us what issue you're experiencing" |
| **Legal/Privacy** | Direct, transparent | "We do NOT sell your data." |
| **Error Messages** | Helpful, blame-free | "Something went wrong. Let's try again." |
| **Success States** | Encouraging, brief | "Gallery shared. Your link is ready." |

Never use exclamation points except in rare, genuinely exciting moments.

### Capitalization

- "DESIGNED FOR YOU"

- "NEED HELP"

#### Title Case for Proper Titles
- "Galerly â€” Where moments become memories"

#### Sentence case for everything else
- Regular content, buttons, descriptions
- Natural, readable, professional

### Word Choice

#### Preferred Words
- **Art** (not content, files, assets)
- **Photography** (not photos, pics, images)
- **Work** (not output, deliverables)
- **Share** (not distribute, deliver)
- **Create** (not build, make)
- **Your** (not the user's, the photographer's)

- **Beautiful** (not amazing, awesome, stunning)
- **Fast** (not quick, rapid, speedy)
- **Yours** (ownership, control)

#### Avoided Words (from user rules)

#### Language of Photography

- Upload, download, share, select

## Typography

### Font System: SF Pro

#### SF Pro Display
- **Use for:** Headlines, hero text, large display (â‰¥20px)
- **Weights Used:**
  - 300 (Light): Hero titles, elegant large text
  - 400 (Regular): Section headers
  - 500 (Medium): Emphasized headings
  - 600 (Semibold): Strong section headers

#### SF Pro Text
- **Use for:** Body copy, UI, readable content (<20px)
- **Weights Used:**
  - 400 (Regular): All body text, navigation, forms
  - 500 (Medium): Primary buttons, emphasized UI
  - 600 (Semibold): Strong emphasis, form headings

### Weight Hierarchy Philosophy

**Lighter = Larger & More Elegant**  
**Heavier = Smaller & More Readable**

- 72px+ hero: Weight 300 (Light)
- 32-48px headers: Weight 300-400 (Light to Regular)
- 16-20px body: Weight 400 (Regular)
- 16-18px buttons: Weight 500 (Medium for CTAs)
- <16px small text: Weight 500-600 (needs weight for legibility)

### Typographic Voice

- **Hero text:** Light, spacious, breathing room
- **Body text:** Regular, highly readable, never bold
- **Emphasis:** Semibold (600), not bold (700)
- **Buttons:** Medium (500) for primary CTAs

## Visual Identity

### Color Palette

#### Primary Colors

```
Science Blue:    #0066CC  (Primary brand, buttons, links, trust)
Shark (Dark):    #1D1D1F  (Headings, primary text, sophistication)
Light Gray:      #F5F5F7  (Backgrounds, subtle elements)
Pure White:      #FFFFFF  (Clean backgrounds, cards)
Coral Accent:    #FF6F61  (Alerts, warnings, emotional touches)
Mint Green:      #98FF98  (Success states, positive actions)
```

#### Color Philosophy

- **Blue dominates:** Professional, trustworthy, creative industry standard
- **Dark text on light:** Maximum readability, photographer-first
- **Gray as canvas:** Let photography be the color
- **Coral sparingly:** Only for urgent or emotional moments
- **Mint rarely:** Success confirmations only

#### Color Usage

- **Buttons Primary:** Blue (#0066CC)
- **Text Primary:** Shark (#1D1D1F)
- **Backgrounds:** Light Gray (#F5F5F7), White (#FFFFFF)
- **Links:** Blue (#0066CC)
- **Alerts/Urgent:** Coral (#FF6F61)
- **Success:** Mint (#98FF98)

### Design Style: Liquid Glass

**Glassmorphism Aesthetic**

- Frosted glass effects with backdrop blur
- Subtle transparency: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.7)
- Soft shadows: `0 8px 32px rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.15)`
- Border radius: 16-28px for cards and containers
- Clean, modern, airy feeling

### Spacing Philosophy

- **Generous white space:** Let content breathe
- **Consistent rhythm:** 4px/8px/16px/24px/32px/48px scale
- **Top padding:** 180px on desktop, 100px mobile
- **Section gaps:** 120px between major sections
- **Card padding:** 24-32px internal spacing

### Border Radius Scale

```
xs: 10px  (small elements)
s:  12px  (buttons, inputs)
m:  16px  (cards)
l:  20px  (large cards)
xl: 28px  (hero cards, major containers)
```

## User Experience Philosophy

### Core UX Principles

#### 1. **Photographer-Centric**
- Photographers are artists, not "users"
- Design for their workflow, not abstract best practices
- Respect their time, craft, and vision

#### 2. **Immediate Clarity**
- No guessing what buttons do
- Clear labels, obvious actions
- "New Gallery" not "Create" or "+"

#### 3. **Minimal Friction**
- No accounts required for clients viewing galleries
- One-link sharing
- Drag-and-drop upload
- Auto-save everywhere

#### 4. **Visual Hierarchy**
- Large, clear numbers for stats (48px, weight 800)
- Scannable cards with glassmorphism
- Generous spacing between actions

#### 5. **Progressive Disclosure**
- Show essentials first
- Advanced features tucked in settings
- Never overwhelm with options

### Interface Language

**Dashboard:**
- "Your photography workspace"
- "Loading your portfolio summary..."
- "Your collections"

**Empty States:**
- "No galleries yet. Create your first collection."
- "Loading your galleries..."
- "Your collections will appear here momentarily."

**Actions:**
- "Create Gallery" (not "New" or "Add")
- "Upload Photos" (not "Add Images")
- "Share Gallery" (not "Send Link")

**Stats:**
- "Total collections created"
- "Total images uploaded"

## Forbidden Language

### Never Use

From user rules, these words/phrases are strictly forbidden:

- Corporate buzzwords: leverage, utilize, synergy, ecosystem
- Hyperbole: revolutionary, game-changer, disruptive, groundbreaking
- Vague promises: seamless, effortless, powerful, robust
- Marketing clichÃ©s: unlock, unleash, empower, transform
- Tech jargon outside docs: optimize, streamline, scalable
- Redundancy: "various different," "completely finish"

### Why
These words:
- Sound insincere and corporate
- Undermine trust with photographers
- Add no real meaning
- Make copy longer without adding value

### Replace With

| Instead of... | Use... |
|--------------|--------|
| "Leverage our platform" | "Use Galerly" |
| "Seamless experience" | "Simple. Fast." |
| "Powerful tools" | "Tools that work." |
| "Revolutionary approach" | "A new way to share photography." |
| "Unlock your potential" | "Share your work beautifully." |
| "Empower photographers" | "Built for photographers." |

## Implementation Guidelines

### For Copywriters

1. **Read the codebase examples** - The best brand voice is already in the existing pages
2. **Write short first drafts** - Then make them shorter
3. **Remove adjectives** - Most aren't needed
4. **Trust periods** - They create rhythm and impact
5. **Read aloud** - If it sounds corporate, rewrite it

### For Designers

1. **Let photography be the star** - Brand is the frame, not the picture
2. **Use space generously** - Cramped = cheap
3. **Stick to the system** - SF Pro fonts, blue buttons, glass effects
4. **Test on real photography** - Not stock photos
5. **Mobile-first** - Most photographers check on phone

### For Developers

1. **Loading states matter** - "Loading your galleries..." not "Loading..."
2. **Error messages** - Helpful, not "Error 404"
3. **Empty states** - Encouraging, not blank
4. **Success feedback** - Brief confirmation, not modal
5. **Keep UI text short** - If it doesn't fit, it's too long

### For Support Team

1. **Never copy-paste templates** - Personalize every response
2. **Use photographer's name** - They're people, not tickets
3. **Assume competence** - They're professionals
4. **Be honest** - If you don't know, say so
5. **Follow up** - Close the loop
